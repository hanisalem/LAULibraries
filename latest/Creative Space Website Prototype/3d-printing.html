3D Printing

Turn your digital designs into physical reality.

Bring Ideas to Life

Our 3D printing station provides LAU students and faculty with the tools to create rapid prototypes, architectural models, custom parts, and artistic pieces. We support multi-material slicing and offer guidance on optimizing your designs for successful prints.

Supported Materials

  • PLA (Polylactic Acid): Ideal for concept models, rapid prototyping, and detailed artistic prints. Highly reliable and environmentally friendly.
  • PETG (Polyethylene Terephthalate Glycol): Stronger and more durable. Excellent for functional parts that require minor flexibility and high impact resistance.
  • TPU (Thermoplastic Polyurethane): A flexible, rubber-like material perfect for wearables, soft grips, and impact-absorbing components.

Getting Started

1. Complete the Required Training
Before using the printers, you must either attend one of our "Intro to 3D Printing" workshops or complete a brief 1-on-1 orientation session with the Creative Space Supervisor.
2. Prepare Your Design File
Export your 3D design as an .STL or .OBJ file. We recommend using our lab's dedicated high-performance workstations to slice your file using UltiMaker Cura or 3DWOX Sindoh.
3. Supervisor Clearance
All prints must be reviewed and cleared by the Creative Space supervisor before the job begins. This helps ensure your print is optimized, safe, and will not fail mid-process. All materials must be purchased through the creative space or pre-approved.

Available Printers

Dual extrusion, large build volume, ideal for complex geometries.
